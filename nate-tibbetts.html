Tibbetts is From Sioux Falls

Born on May 15, 1977, Nate Tibbetts, now 46, entered this world in the heart of South Dakota, America. His father, the legendary Fred Tibbetts, and mother Micki, had no idea they were raising a future basketball sensation.

Nate’s basketball journey kicked off during his school days. He first showed off his skills at Jefferson High School and later at Sioux Falls Roosevelt High School.

After Post high school, Nate continued his basketball odyssey by playing for the University of South Dakota Athletics

But here’s the tearjerker: every step of the way, Nate had his dad, Fred, by his side, guiding him and showing him the path to success.

The story takes a somber turn in 2008 when the basketball world lost a true legend. Fred Tibbetts, the man behind Jefferson and Roosevelt’s more than a dozen championship titles, lost his battle with colon cancer

Nate’s Basketball Career 

Nate Tibbetts is a basketball legend in the North Central Conference, having the highest number of assists in its 88-year history. He gave out 465 assists, which is about 6.5 assists per game during conference matches, leading the Coyotes to three back-to-back NCC championships from 1999 to 2001.

At the University of South Dakota (USD), Nate’s name is etched in stone. He’s second in career assists with 678 and steals with 215. It’s like saying he’s the second-best ever at USD in setting up plays and taking the ball away from opponents.

Back in 2001, Nate earned all-NCC honors and was a star in the NCC all-tournament team. He played 112 games, scoring 572 points (about 5.1 points per game) and grabbing 321 rebounds (around 2.9 rebounds per game). He was also a sharpshooter, making 55 percent of his shots.

In 2011, he became a coach and led a group of D-League players to a bronze medal at the Pan American Games, making it the first medal for the USA in basketball since 1999.

Nate started his coaching journey as an assistant coach at the University of Sioux Falls from 2001 to 2005. Then, he joined the Sioux Falls Skyforce in the D-League, eventually becoming the head coach after two years.

Now, Nate is the head coach of the Phoenix Mercury, and he’s previously been an assistant coach for the NBA’s Portland Trailblazers. Before that, he spent two seasons with the Cleveland Cavaliers as an assistant coach and two more seasons as the head coach of the NBA Development League’s Tulsa 66ers
