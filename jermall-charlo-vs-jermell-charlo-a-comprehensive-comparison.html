Jermall Charlo vs. Jermell Charlo: A Comprehensive Comparison

Two boxers who have stood out like none others in the world of professional boxing are siblings Jermall and Jermell Charlo. In this article, we aim to provide a detailed analysis and review of both talented boxers' careers, achievements, and performance so far in their careers.

Background and Early Career

Jermall Charlo

Jermall Charlo (29-0, 21 KOs) had a successful amateur career with an impressive record of 65 wins and started his way into boxing. He turned professional in 2008 and remains unbeaten, scoring 32-0. He has stopped his opponent an impressive 22 times. Other highlights for which he is most famous are winning the IBF super welterweight title in 2015 over Cornelius Bundrage and then moving up to middleweight, defeating Hugo Centeno Jr. for the interim WBC middleweight belt in 2018. Jermall has defended his middleweight crown five times with ease.

Jermell Charlo

Similarly to Jermell Charlo, he had an excellent amateur career before debuting professionally. 31-0, 15 KOs, and the super welterweight lineal champion. Jermell (35-1, 18 KOs) went on to win and defend the junior middleweight crown inside the ring with wins like a first-round knockout of Erickson Lubin, followed by a unanimous decision against Juan Macias Montiel in 2021.

Professional Experience and Achievements

Jermall Charlo

Jermall Charlo's professional record justifies his presence at the top of a middleweight division he has owned since 2018. This unsullied record, along with 22 knockout wins, speaks to his incredible punching power and ring generalship. He most notably earned his way while remaining the undefeated king of middleweight over some very tough contenders, showing he could be a force to reckon with in the division.

Jermell Charlo

Jermell Charlo also has a solid professional record, with all of his 30 wins coming by knockouts. As the reigning super welterweight champion with several title defenses under his belt, Pride shows just how consistent and skillful he is in fights. His adaptability with a variety of opponents leads to win after win, continuing his reign as one of the top fighters in the super welterweight division.

Fighting Style and Skillset

Jermall Charlo

Jermall Charlo is famous for his unique movement, which showcases a perfect blend of punching variety and exquisite counterboxing. Identified as a technical and powerful boxer, he can dictate the tempo of his fight while landing significant punches. He has the kind of knockout power and technical skills that make him a threat in every fight at middleweight.

Jermell Charlo

Jermell, however, is an exciting speedster who tries to pick his shots and combine them into explosive flurries of action. He has the presence in the ring and can adjust to every fight needed, which is why he became a super welterweight champion. His ability to dart in and out with surprising speed while moving off angle has kept Jermell one step ahead of his opponents and scored his big wins.

Head-to-Head Comparison

Charlo brothers have accomplished plenty in professional boxing, emerging as champions in their respective weight classes. When you match his dominance at 160, along with his technical skill and power to go for the knockout, Jermall Charlo is a formidable presence in elite company. On the other hand, Jermell Charlo has established himself as an undisputed super welterweight champion, and his impressive fight style only proves that he can be a dominant figure at 154 lbs.

No matter what happens from here, the Charlo brothers have distinctly made their mark in professional boxing as two individual skill sets. From Jermall's iron-fisted grip on the middleweight division to Jermell holding steady as the super welterweight king, The Charlo Brothers remains consistent in impressing fans with top-level performances in their unique axis inside that squared circle.
