Brock Lesnar is an American professional wrestler and former mixed martial artist. He is also a former amateur wrestler and professional football player.

Brock Lesnar Biography

Brock Lesnar was born on July 12, 1977, in Webster, South Dakota, in the United States of America.

Brock Lesnar enrolled at a local high school before he proceeded to the Lassen Community College and Bismarck State College. He is also a graduate of the University of Minnesota.

In 2000, Brock Lesnar became the youngest ‘WWE’ champion at the age of 25.

At age 17, Lesnar joined the Army National Guard and he was assigned to an office job after his red-green colorblindness was deemed hazardous to his desire to work with explosives. He was discharged after failing a computer typing test and later worked for a construction company.

He has three siblings who are Troy, Chad, and Brandi Lesnar.

Brock Lesnar is now signed by WWE and has a deal with them.

Brock Lesnar started his professional career as a wrestler two decades ago when he debuted on WWF television on March 18, 2002. He debuted as a heel, attacking Al Snow, Maven, and Spike Dudley.

Brock Lesnar says he is done with the UFC and won’t be changing his mind. The 44-year-old is a former UFC heavyweight champion but hasn’t fought since defeating Mark Hunt at UFC 200. That result was later overturned after Lesnar tested positive for clomiphene.

Brock Lesnar is estimated to have a net worth of $25 million. He is one of the richest wrestlers in the world. He has over the years been earning a lot from WWE. His hard work is paying off.

Brock Lesnar is a married man. He exchanged marriage vows with Sable in 2006. They have been together ever since.

Sable is the wife of Brock Lesnar. They have been united as a family since 2006 and producing children.

Brock Lesnar has four children from his marriage with Sable. He is the father of Mya Lynn Lesnar, Luke Lesnar, Turk Lesnar, and Duke Lesnar.

Brock Lesnar has a daughter who is called Mya Lynn Lesnar. She is one of America’s leading shot-putters, having been crowned as Minnesota State Champion.
