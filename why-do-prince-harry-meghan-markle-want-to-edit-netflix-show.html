Why Do Prince Harry And Meghan Markle Want To Edit Their Netflix Show?

Prior to the Queen’s death, the royal couple had plenty of projects at near completion, Meghan Markle’s podcast on Spotify, Archetypes, Harry’s tell-all memoir, and the filming for their docuseries with Netflix, which was well underway. However, it appears that the Netflix show might be “shelved indefinitely.”

According to Page Six, Prince Harry and Meghan Markle are "panicked" and trying to "tone down" their upcoming Netflix show, but they are receiving the cold shoulder from the streaming giant's executives, who think big adjustments may lead to the production being canceled altogether.

Citing the source, the couple is “having second thoughts on their own story” but they are at differences with Netflix and even their own production crew as they backtrack on what they intend to reveal to the public in their upcoming docuseries.

The pair purportedly wants to remove parts of the documentary, which they have been recording for more than a year, but Netflix executives are allegedly supporting the filmmakers who want to keep the content.

Another source of the publication also noted, “Harry and Meghan panicked about trying to tone down even the most basic language. But it’s their story, from their own mouths.”

Previous reports revealed that the Duke and Duchess of Sussex wanted to edit the docuseries in the wake of the Queen’s death, as it is believed to contain a number of bombshells about Prince William, Kate Middleton, King Charles III, and Queen Consort Camilla.

While Prince Harry and Meghan Markle – who has landed a deal that is said to be worth a whopping $100 million — want the Netflix show to launch next year, the streaming platform has long desired to release it immediately following the fifth season of The Crown.
