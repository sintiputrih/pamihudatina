What Happened to Drake Bell?

February 2024 · 3 minute read

Drake Bell, best known for his role in the hit Nickelodeon series "Drake & Josh," has experienced a tumultuous journey marked by significant career achievements, legal troubles, family matters, and personal revelations.

Early Career and Rise to Fame

Drake Bell made his foray into the entertainment industry in the early 1990s, with early roles in popular TV shows such as "Home Improvement" and "Seinfeld."

His breakthrough came with his role in the sketch comedy series "The Amanda Show" (1999–2002), alongside Amanda Bynes. Subsequently, he and his co-star Josh Peck landed the lead roles in "Drake & Josh" (2004–2007), a beloved sitcom in which they played teenage stepsiblings.

Bell's success in these shows earned him critical acclaim and numerous accolades, including ten Kids' Choice Awards, a Teen Choice Award, and a Young Artist Award.

Legal Troubles and Controversies

In recent years, Drake Bell has been embroiled in legal troubles and controversies that have garnered widespread media attention.

In June 2021, he faced charges of attempted endangering children and disseminating matter harmful to juveniles, to which he initially pleaded not guilty.

Subsequently, he virtually appeared in court, where he agreed to a plea deal and was sentenced to two years of probation, 200 hours of community service, and prohibited from contacting the victim.

During the hearing, a 19-year-old victim publicly accused Bell of grooming her from the age of 12 and sexually abusing her when she was 15.

In 2024, during an interview on "Quiet on Set: The Dark Side of Kids TV," Bell revealed for the first time that he was sexually abused by his former acting and dialogue coach Brian Peck while working as a child actor at Nickelodeon.

He disclosed that the abuse was "extensive" and "brutal," shedding light on a deeply troubling aspect of his past.

Personal Struggles and Family Matters

Apart from legal issues, Bell has faced personal struggles and family matters.

In 2005, he was involved in a serious car accident that resulted in severe injuries, including a fractured neck, broken jaw, loss of teeth, and deep facial lacerations, necessitating extensive medical treatment and recovery.

In terms of family matters, Bell revealed that he had been married to Janet Von Schmeling since 2018, and the couple had a son.

Yet, their relationship faced challenges, leading to their separation in September 2022. Janet filed for divorce on April 20, 2023, citing irreconcilable differences, marking a significant development in Bell's personal life.

Professional Evolution and Music Career

In addition to his acting career, Drake Bell has pursued a music career, contributing to the soundtrack of "Drake & Josh" and releasing several studio albums.

Notably, in 2017, he released a new song titled "Run Away" from his EP "Honest," signaling a shift towards a more music-centric focus. His musical endeavors have showcased his versatility and evolution as an artist, allowing him to connect with fans through his music.

Relationship Dynamics and Controversies with Co-Stars

While Bell achieved widespread fame through his on-screen partnership with Josh Peck in "Drake & Josh," their off-screen relationship has been marked by ups and downs.

Their publicized rift, which notably unfolded when Peck did not invite Bell to his wedding in 2017, became a topic of public interest.

Bell's reaction to this omission, expressed through social media posts and subsequent reconciliations, shed light on the complex dynamics between the two former co-stars.

 

On the whole, Drake Bell's life and career have been shaped by a series of highs and lows, including legal challenges, personal revelations, family matters, and professional evolution.

Despite the controversies and personal struggles, his enduring impact on the entertainment industry through his iconic roles and musical endeavors remains an integral part of his legacy.
